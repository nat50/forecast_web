About Dry Eye Disease

Understanding Dry Eye Syndrome

Dry eye disease is a common condition affecting millions worldwide, characterized by insufficient tear production or poor tear quality.

Risk Factors

Extended screen time, poor sleep quality, contact lens use, aging, and certain medications can increase your risk of developing dry eye syndrome.

Common Symptoms

Symptoms include eye discomfort, burning sensation, redness, light sensitivity, blurred vision, and a gritty feeling in the eyes.

Prevention

Regular breaks from screens, adequate hydration, proper sleep, and using blue-light filters can help reduce your risk of dry eye disease.
